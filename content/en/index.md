## Structure
An EAS is the most simple company.  
The legal rapresentative must be permanent resident of Paraguay. We offer two solutions for this: or I will be your appointed legal rapresentative for an annual fee or you get a second residency in Paraguay. To become a resident you need two visits in Paraguay and a fee of about 1000 USD. We have first hand experience with this and you will be 100% approved in a few months.  
You must provide an address in paraguay for the office (my place is available for this if I will be the legal rapresentative, otherwise there is a shared location).  
Shareholders can be of any country , individuals or companies.  

## Taxation
An EAS has 3 taxes:  

- IVA (vat) 10% but it's 0 for us since we invoice out of the country with a semi automated system.  
- IRE (income) 3% on the total income if you don't deduct national expenses (up to 250.000 USD) or 10% if you deduct expenses.  
- IDU (dividend) 8% if the shareholder is a resident, 15% if not resident, paid only on the distributed profits of the company.   

You will be not perceived as an offshore incorporated business, with all the annexed consequences to invoice EU/US clients.

## Banking
Paraguay is one of the few countries in the world not part of the CRS system (there is no exchange of info with other countries, your info will remain private at the bank).  
Opening a local business bank account for your company is pretty simple.  
Online banks like Revolut and Wise are not available but there are some good alternative one of which supports local payment details in various ares like Wise.  
Stripe for a paraguayan companies it's not supported, but there are good alternative available with medium market fee.  
Alternatively, you can create a US/UK/CA subsidiary company owned by the paraguayan company, for 400 USD to process credit card payments. We have first hand experience with this and can handle all the process that is very easy.  
We can also help you to accept bitcoin and USDT payments with a self custodial solution.
